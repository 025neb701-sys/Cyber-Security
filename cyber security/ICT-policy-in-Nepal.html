About ICT-policy-in-Nepal

Nepal's ICT Policy refers to the set of government strategies, guidelines, and regulations that govern the development and use of Information and Communication Technology in the country. The most significant framework is the Digital Nepal Framework (2019), which outlines eight digital domains: Digital Infrastructure, Digital Economy, Digital Governance, Digital Agriculture, Digital Health, Digital Education, Digital Tourism, and Digital Finance. The goal is to transform Nepal into a knowledge-based economy, reduce the digital divide between urban and rural areas, and leverage technology for inclusive development. The Nepal Telecommunication Authority (NTA) oversees telecommunications regulation, licensing, and broadband expansion. For individuals, the ICT Policy directly affects access to the internet, e-government services, and digital education. Initiatives like e-Government services allow citizens to pay taxes, check land records, and apply for documents online, saving time and reducing corruption. The policy also emphasises digital literacy — equipping citizens, especially in rural Nepal, with the skills to use digital tools effectively. Broadband expansion policies aim to bring affordable internet to all 77 districts. From a cybersecurity perspective, the ICT Policy mandates that government systems must meet certain security standards, and it outlines a roadmap for establishing a National CERT (Computer Emergency Response Team) to handle cyber incidents at the national level. ★Importance A clear ICT policy ensures that technology development benefits all citizens — not just those in Kathmandu. It creates a legal framework for investment, protects consumers, and ensures government digital systems are accountable and secure. Without policy, technology adoption becomes chaotic and exclusionary. e-Governance Example: A citizen in Jumla can now apply for a citizenship certificate online through the Department of National ID and Civil Registration portal, without travelling to Kathmandu. Digital Education Example: Under the ICT Policy, schools in remote districts receive computers and internet connectivity, allowing students to access digital learning resources. NTA Role: When an internet service provider raises prices unfairly, the NTA can investigate and enforce regulations to protect consumers.
