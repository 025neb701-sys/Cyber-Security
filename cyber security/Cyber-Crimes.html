About Cyber Crimes

Cyber crimes are criminal activities carried out using computers, networks, or the internet either as tools or as targets. They are broadly categorised into: Crimes against individuals (cyberstalking, cyberbullying, identity theft, phishing), Crimes against property (hacking, data theft, online fraud, ransomware attacks), and Crimes against governments (cyber espionage, attacks on critical infrastructure). Unlike traditional crimes, cyber crimes transcend geographical boundaries — a hacker in one country can attack a bank in another instantly, and both the anonymity of the internet and the lack of international legal harmonisation make detection and prosecution difficult. In Nepal, the Cyber Bureau of Nepal Police is the primary agency investigating cyber crimes. The most common cyber crimes affecting individuals today are phishing (fraudulent emails or messages tricking you into revealing passwords or financial information), online fraud (fake e-commerce sites, lottery scams), social media account hacking, and cyberbullying (harassment through digital platforms). Ransomware attacks, where malware encrypts a victim's files and demands payment for decryption, have also surged globally. Understanding the methods criminals use is the first step in protection. Common preventive measures include verifying the sender's identity before clicking links, enabling two-factor authentication (2FA), not sharing personal information on public platforms, and regularly monitoring bank statements for unauthorised transactions. ★Importance Cyber crimes cause enormous financial, emotional, and reputational harm. Globally, cybercrime costs trillions of dollars annually. In Nepal, reported cases of online fraud and social media-related crimes have risen sharply. Awareness is the most effective weapon against cyber criminals. Phishing Example: Sita receives an email appearing to be from her bank saying "Your account will be suspended — click here to verify." The link leads to a fake website that captures her username and password. Her account is then drained. Cyberbullying Example: A student creates a fake Instagram account using a classmate's photos and posts humiliating content. This is both a cyber crime and a violation of the Individual Privacy Act in Nepal. Ransomware Example: A hospital's computer systems are infected with ransomware. All patient records are encrypted and attackers demand $50,000 in Bitcoin. Patient care is disrupted until either the ransom is paid or systems are restored from backups. Online Fraud Example: A person advertises a non-existent laptop on a Facebook group, collects payment via eSewa, and disappears. This is online financial fraud prosecutable under IT Act 2063.
